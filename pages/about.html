WHO WE ARE

Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is the space agency of India. The organisation is involved in science, engineering and technology to harvest the benefits of outer space for India and the mankind.

ISRO is a major constituent of the Department of Space (DOS), Government of India. The department executes the Indian Space Programme primarily through various Centres or units within ISRO.

OUR GENESIS

1962

INCOSPAR (Indian National Committee for Space Research) was set up by the Government of India, as envisioned by Dr. Vikram A Sarabhai, the father of the Indian Space Programme.

1969

ISRO was formed on August 15, 1969 and superseded INCOSPAR with an expanded role to harness space technology for national development.

1972

The Department of Space (DOS) was established, and ISRO was brought under DOS to coordinate all space activities.

LAUNCH CAPABILITIES

ISRO has developed satellite launch vehicles, PSLV and GSLV, to place satellites in the required orbits. These indigenous launch vehicles have enabled India to achieve self-reliance in satellite launch capability.

SCIENCE & EDUCATION

Alongside its technological advancement, ISRO contributes to science and science education in the country. Various dedicated research centres and autonomous institutions for remote sensing, astronomy and astrophysics, atmospheric sciences and space sciences in general function under the aegis of Department of Space.

ISRO's own Lunar and interplanetary missions along with other scientific projects encourage and promote science education, apart from providing valuable data to the scientific community which in turn enriches science.

OUR CENTRES

ISRO has its headquarters in Bengaluru. Its activities are spread across various centres and units:

VSSC - Vikram Sarabhai Space Centre

Thiruvananthapuram

Launch Vehicles development and production

URSC - U R Rao Satellite Centre

Bengaluru

Satellite design and development

SDSC SHAR - Satish Dhawan Space Centre

Sriharikota

Integration and launching of satellites and launch vehicles

LPSC - Liquid Propulsion Systems Centre

Valiamala & Bengaluru

Development of liquid stages including cryogenic stage

SAC - Space Applications Centre

Ahmedabad

Sensors for Communication and Remote Sensing satellites and application aspects

NRSC - National Remote Sensing Centre

Hyderabad

Remote Sensing satellite data reception, processing and dissemination
