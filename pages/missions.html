CHANDRAYAAN SERIES

Moon Exploration

Chandrayaan-1 (2008)

First Indian lunar probe. It famously made the groundbreaking discovery of water molecules on the lunar surface.

Chandrayaan-2 (2019)

A complex mission featuring an Orbiter, Lander, and Rover. The Orbiter is still fully operational and continues to provide immense scientific data.

Chandrayaan-3 (2023)

A historic achievement where India became the first nation to successfully land a spacecraft on the lunar South Pole.

MANGALYAAN

Mars Orbiter Mission (2013)

India's first interplanetary mission, showcasing ISRO's exceptional capabilities in frugal engineering and precision navigation across millions of kilometers.

  • First Asian nation to reach Mars orbit
  • First nation in the world to succeed on its maiden attempt
  • Cost: ₹450 crore (famously less than the Hollywood movie "Gravity")
  • Provided unprecedented images and data of the Martian surface

ADITYA-L1

Solar Observatory (2023)

India's first dedicated scientific mission to study the Sun, positioned advantageously at Lagrange Point L1, about 1.5 million km from Earth.

Objective 1 Study Solar Corona
Objective 2 Analyze Solar Winds
Objective 3 Monitor Space Weather
