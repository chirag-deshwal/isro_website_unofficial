HEAVY LIFT

LVM3

Launch Vehicle Mark-3 ("The Bahubali")

ISRO's heaviest and most capable launch vehicle. Designed to carry heavy communication satellites into Geosynchronous Transfer Orbit (GTO) and large payloads to Low Earth Orbit (LEO).

HEIGHT

43.5 m

STAGES

3

PAYLOAD (LEO)

8,000 kg

PAYLOAD (GTO)

4,000 kg

Notable Missions:

Chandrayaan-3, OneWeb India, Gaganyaan (upcoming)

WORKHORSE

PSLV

Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle

ISRO's most reliable launcher with over 50 consecutive successful missions. It holds the distinguished record for consistency and famously launched 104 satellites in a single mission.

HEIGHT

44 m

STAGES

4

PAYLOAD (SSO)

1,750 kg

SUCCESS RATE

95%+

Notable Records:

104 satellites in one launch (2017), Chandrayaan-1, Mangalyaan

GEOSYNCHRONOUS

GSLV

Geosynchronous Satellite Launch Vehicle

Designed mainly to deliver communication satellites to the highly elliptical Geosynchronous Transfer Orbit (GTO). It features India's indigenous cryogenic upper stage engine.

HEIGHT

49.13 m

STAGES

3

PAYLOAD (GTO)

2,500 kg

UPPER STAGE

CE-7.5 Cryogenic

RAPID LAUNCH

SSLV

Small Satellite Launch Vehicle

The newest addition to ISRO's fleet, designed to provide launch-on-demand services for the burgeoning commercial small satellite market. Key features include low cost and extraordinarily rapid turnaround time.

HEIGHT

34 m

STAGES

3

PAYLOAD (LEO)

500 kg

TURNAROUND

72 Hours
